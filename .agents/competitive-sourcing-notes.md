# Sixam - competitive sourcing notes

Date: 2026-06-26

## What to study next

Sixam must track three competitive clusters:

1. Asian anti-UV practicality: China, Taiwan, Japan.
2. Scandinavian premium minimalism: Denmark, Sweden, Norway, Finland.
3. Premium performance references: On, Hoka, Lululemon, Vuori.

## Asian anti-UV cluster

### Key references

- Beneunder: strong on UV jackets, cooling, face/neck coverage, UPF claims, urban/outdoor lifestyle.
- OhSunny: fashion-oriented sun protection, proprietary Twilight fabric, UPF50+, strong feature storytelling.
- UV100: Taiwan specialist, full-system sun protection with jackets, hats, masks, sleeves, cooling designs.
- Uniqlo AIRism / UV Protection: mass-market practicality, breathable everyday UV layers.
- Muji UV Cut: quiet minimalism, useful daily UV pieces, no loud branding.
- Bosideng UV line: large Chinese apparel player entering UV protection.

### What Asian competitors do well

- They treat sun protection as a complete system: hoodie, visor, sleeves, mask, gloves, jacket, cooling.
- They create practical details: face cover, neck cover, thumb holes, packability, cooling yarn, quick-dry fabrics.
- They educate around UV ratings and testing.
- They make UPF a daily habit, not only a medical niche.
- They are strong at "problem solving" product design.

### What Sixam should borrow

- Product practicality.
- Clear UPF proof.
- Cooling / sweat / quick-dry language.
- Accessories ecosystem: cap, sleeves, neck cover, towel, band.
- Supplier discipline: ask for SGS, Intertek, ARPANSA or equivalent reports.

### What Sixam should avoid

- Looking like face-cover commuter gear.
- Looking cheap or marketplace-driven.
- Too many features above the fold.
- Medical fear-based copy.

## Scandinavian cluster

### Key references

- Cuera: Danish padel apparel, on/off-court lifestyle, quick-dry, breathable, recycled fabrics.
- Kalani: Swedish premium sun protective fashion, Italian fabrics, breathable, quick-dry, stylish outdoor positioning.
- Haglöfs L.I.M: minimalist Scandinavian technical performance, lightweight, packable, sun protection.
- Nordic outdoor brands broadly: simple design, smart materials, quiet durability.

### What Scandinavian competitors do well

- They make technical clothing feel premium through restraint.
- They use minimal copy and strong product naming.
- They balance performance, aesthetics and durability.
- They avoid over-selling.

### What Sixam should borrow

- Minimal layout.
- Fewer buttons.
- Less copy.
- Product architecture by use case.
- Premium restraint instead of loud "tech bro" language.

## Premium performance web patterns

### On / Hoka / Lululemon style lessons

- Big hero, short line, strong visual.
- Product benefit in very few words.
- Specs are secondary, not the hero.
- Pages feel expensive because they are sparse.
- CTA is clear and repeated only at natural decision points.

## Implications for Sixam

Sixam should combine:

- Practical anti-UV functionality inspired by Asian product depth.
- Restrained premium design inspired by Scandinavian and performance brands.
- Premium performance pricing and polish.

Internal shorthand only:

"Asian functionality" and "Scandinavian restraint" are useful research labels, not customer-facing copy.

Updated site rule:

Do not explain too much. Make the product feel obvious.

Better copy direction:

- Des vêtements anti-UV premium pour les sports au soleil.
- Protéger du soleil sans perdre l'allure.
- Le soleil, la sueur, les vêtements qui collent.
- Protection solaire. Tissu respirant. Coupe soignée.
- Pour jouer, courir, s'entraîner et voyager quand la journée se passe dehors.

Avoid:

- La fonction asiatique. La retenue scandinave. Le prix premium.
- Conçu pour padel, running, training et voyages au soleil.
- Over-listing sports in the hero. Sixam should feel broader than a padel niche.
